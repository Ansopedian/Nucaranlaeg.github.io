Death and Dying

Wounds

A creature takes wounds whenever they would take damage that would leave them below 0 HP or they are hit by critical hits. Any excess damage (and all damage from critical hits) is divided by 5+Con (round fractions up) and taken as wounds. Critical hits do not increase damage dealt.

For a creature has 9 health, +2 Con, and is hit by a 12 damage crit. It drops to 0 health and takes (3+12)/(5+2)=15/7=3 wounds. It only falls unconscious if it fails its death saving throw.

Whenever a creature takes one or more wound, they must make a death saving throw. On a failure, they fall unconscious.

Death Saving Throws

Death saving throws have a DC equal to the total number of wounds the creature has. The only modifier to death saving throws is the creature's constitution.

Unconsciousness

When a creature is unconscious, on their turn they must roll a death saving throw. On a failure, the creature dies.

An unconscious creature can be stabilized by rolling a natural 20 on a death saving throw, recieving healing equal to one point per wound (for that purpose - it heals no hit points), or having an ally succeed on a healing check with DC 5+wounds.

Advantage

Stacking Advantage

Advantage now stacks! To determine your total Advantage, add the number of sources of Advantage you have and subtract the sources of Disadvantage. If the total is +2 or more, add the total to your roll. If the total is -2 or less, subtract the total from your roll.

Feat - Overwhelming Advantage

If you have at least one source of Advantage on a roll and more Disadvantages than Advantages, subtract 2 from the number of Disadvantages you are suffering. This cannot cause you to gain Advantage. If a creature is making any roll, you have imposed Disadvantage on that roll, and that creature has more Advantages than Disadvantages, subtract 2 from the number of Advantages they have. This cannot cause them to suffer Disadvantage.
